Biography

Virat Kohli (born 5 November 1988) is an Indian cricketer and former captain of the Indian national team. He is regarded as one of the best batsmen in the world. Kohli was born and raised in Delhi and represents the Delhi cricket team in domestic cricket. He has played for the Royal Challengers Bangalore in the Indian Premier League (IPL) since the tournament's inception in 2008.

Kohli made his international debut in 2008 and has since achieved numerous records and accolades. He has received several awards, including the Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy for ICC Cricketer of the Year in 2017 and 2018. Under his captaincy, the Indian cricket team achieved notable success, including the 2018-19 tour of Australia, where India became the first Asian team to win a Test series in Australia.

Cricket Career

Virat Kohli has achieved remarkable success in his cricket career. He has consistently displayed exceptional batting skills and a strong work ethic. Kohli holds numerous records, including being the fastest player to score 8,000, 9,000, 10,000, and 11,000 runs in One Day Internationals (ODIs).

He has received several awards and accolades, including the prestigious Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy for ICC Cricketer of the Year on multiple occasions. Kohli has been an integral part of the Indian cricket team's success, leading them to victories in various series and tournaments.
